# Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) Roadmap

GRC professionals are the strategists and auditors of the cyber world. They translate complex technical risks into business language, ensuring the organization stays compliant with laws while maintaining a robust security posture.

GRC is where technology meets the boardroom.

    * **Risk:** The "What Ifs"‚Äîidentifying and measuring potential harm.
    * **Compliance:** The "Musts"‚Äîadhering to laws and standards.
* **Security Principles:** Understanding the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability).
* **Technical Literacy:** Basic understanding of networking, cloud, and identity management to effectively audit them.

## üü° Level 2: Frameworks & Standards
You don't need to reinvent the wheel. GRC relies on established "playbooks."
* **General Security Frameworks:** **NIST CSF** (Cybersecurity Framework), **ISO/IEC 27001**, and **CIS Critical Security Controls**.
* **Regulatory Compliance:** * **GDPR:** Data privacy for EU citizens.
    * **HIPAA:** Healthcare data security (US).
    * **PCI DSS:** Security for credit card processing.
    * **SOC 2:** Trust service criteria for service organizations.
* **Policy Writing:** Learning to draft clear, enforceable Acceptable Use Policies (AUP) and Incident Response Plans.

---

## üü† Level 3: Risk Management & Auditing
Moving from "knowing the rules" to "checking the work."
* **Risk Assessments:** Identifying assets, threats, and vulnerabilities to calculate Risk = (Threat √ó Vulnerability √ó Asset Value).
* **Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM):** Evaluating the security of vendors and partners before sharing data.
* **Internal Auditing:** Conducting "walkthroughs" and evidence collection to verify that controls (like MFA or backups) are actually working.
* **Business Continuity Planning (BCP):** Creating plans to keep the business running during a disaster or ransomware attack.

---

## üî¥ Level 4: Strategic Leadership & Automation
* **GRC Tooling & Automation:** Moving away from spreadsheets to automated platforms.
    * *Tools:* **ServiceNow GRC**, **LogicGate**, **AuditBoard**, or **Archer**.
* **Continuous Monitoring:** Implementing dashboards that show real-time compliance status.
* **Boardroom Communication:** Learning to present cyber risks in terms of "Dollars and Cents" rather than "Bits and Bytes."
* **AI Governance:** Creating policies for the ethical and secure use of AI within the company.

## üìù The GRC Professional's Code
* **Business Enabler, Not Blocker:** The goal isn't to say "No," but to say "Yes, safely."
* **Integrity is Paramount:** An auditor's most valuable asset is their honesty.
* **Continuous Learning:** Regulations (like the EU AI Act) change constantly; your knowledge must evolve with them.
